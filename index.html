About

From an early age, I noticed a strong inclination towards technology in myself. I often found myself in situations where people would ask me: 'Did you see the new device that was launched?' These innovations fascinated me. I was eager to know how they worked, what was behind them—wait, computers have a language? Hold on, several? My interest was always focused on what was behind it all. I even started formatting relatives' phones, and that was my small beginning.

In 2013, my mother enrolled me in a computer and typing course, where I stood out. I even finished the course before the colleagues who had already been attending. However, despite completing the course, a management change in the company prevented me from receiving my certificate, and that discouraged me.

I completed my elementary education at Escola Estadual Israel de Amorim, and in 9th grade, a professor from a Federal Institute invited us to take the entrance exam for an integrated high school program with one of three available courses: Systems Development, Chemistry, and Agriculture. Although I had never been a top student academically, I always loved challenges, as they motivated me to do my best. To my surprise, I was accepted and completed my high school with a technical degree in Systems Development, which broadened my view of new opportunities and ambitions.

Currently, I am pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science, with the prospect of enrolling in a new course or a graduate program. My journey at a Federal Institute has provided me with enriching experiences, such as trips I wouldn’t have had outside the academic environment, and has motivated me to always seek new opportunities and fully dedicate myself to what I truly want.
